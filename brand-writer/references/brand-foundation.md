# Kontext.dev Brand Foundation

## Company Identity

**What we are**: The infrastructure layer that takes agents from demo to deployment.

**What we do**: Provide the auth, permissions, and observability that agents need to work in production—not just in demos.

**The gap we close**: Every agent demo works. Getting that agent to production—with real OAuth, real permissions, real observability—takes weeks. Kontext closes that gap in minutes.

**Deeper narrative** (for thought leadership, not headlines): The internet is evolving from an Attention Economy (monetizing eyeballs) to an Intention Economy (monetizing outcomes). Agents will run for hours or days, executing tasks autonomously. Current infrastructure assumes humans in short-lived sessions. We're building what's missing.

## Brand Archetype: The Innovative Disruptor

We challenge conventional security approaches. We bring fresh thinking, agility, and optimism to a space dominated by fear-based messaging. We make people stop, think differently, and feel empowered rather than scared.

## Voice Principles

### Optimistic, Not Fear-Based
- Lead with possibility, not threats
- Frame security as enabling outcomes, not blocking attacks
- Use "unlock," "enable," "empower" over "protect," "defend," "prevent"
- Never use FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt)

### Technically Confident, Not Arrogant
- Speak with authority grounded in genuine expertise
- Acknowledge complexity without hiding behind jargon
- Be direct about what we know and honest about limitations
- Never claim "100% secure" or make unsubstantiated promises

### Clear and Accessible
- Translate complex concepts into plain language
- Use concrete examples over abstract descriptions
- Avoid buzzwords unless they add precision
- Write for smart people who aren't security specialists

### Conversational and Human
- Write like a knowledgeable friend, not a corporation
- Use "you" and "your" to address readers directly
- Keep sentences punchy; vary rhythm
- Inject personality without being flippant

### Outcome-Focused
- Lead with what users can achieve, not what we built
- Quantify impact when possible ("in minutes, not days")
- Show the transformation: before state → after state
- Make the customer the hero of the story

## Culture Values (Reflected in Voice)

1. **Integrity**: We say what we mean. No exaggeration, no spin.
2. **Transparency**: We explain our reasoning. We share the hard truths.
3. **Autonomy & Innovation**: We challenge the status quo. We question assumptions.
4. **Technical Excellence**: We care about craft. Quality over shortcuts.
5. **Intellectual Curiosity**: We ask "why" and "what if." We explore edges.
6. **Long-Term View**: We build relationships, not transactions.

## Core Messaging Framework

### The Problem (lead with this)
Every agent demo works. But shipping that agent to production means:
- Rebuilding OAuth flows for every integration
- Figuring out permissions and scopes from scratch
- No visibility into what the agent actually does
- Weeks of plumbing before any real value

Developers keep solving the same problems. It's the gap between "it works on my machine" and "it works in production."

### The Solution (Kontext)
Kontext is the infrastructure that closes the gap:
- **Connect**: Hosted OAuth to GitHub, Slack, Linear, and more. No redirect flows to build.
- **Observe**: See every tool call, latency, success/failure in one dashboard.
- **Control**: Set permissions, approvals, and policies. Revoke access instantly.
- **Ship**: Go from zero to governed agent in minutes, not weeks.

### The Promise
"From demo to deployment. For agents."

Supporting messages:
- "Your agent's first real API call. In 10 minutes."
- "Connect to real APIs. See every call. Control what's allowed."
- "Stop rebuilding auth for every agent."

### For Thought Leadership (deeper narrative)
The internet is evolving from an Attention Economy to an Intention Economy. Agents will run for hours or days, taking actions on our behalf. Current infrastructure—built for humans in short browser sessions—doesn't support this. Kontext is building the missing layer: identity, auth, and governance designed for agents from day one.

## Vocabulary Preferences

### Use
- "agents" (not "bots" or "AI assistants")
- "demo to deployment" / "demo to production" (our core frame)
- "ship" (developers ship, they don't "deploy solutions")
- "connect, observe, control" (the product pillars)
- "governed" (implies control without restriction)
- "tool calls" (technical precision)
- "in minutes" (speed emphasis)
- "the gap" (between demo and production)

### Use sparingly (thought leadership only)
- "trust layer" (accurate but abstract—explain first)
- "agentic web" (insider language—use only with context)
- "Intention Economy" (for deeper narrative pieces)

### Avoid
- "secure" as standalone adjective (too vague)
- "cutting-edge," "next-gen," "revolutionary" (empty hype)
- "hackers," "threats," "attacks" as primary framing
- "just" or "simply" (can feel patronizing)
- Passive voice when active is clearer
- "identity security" (too broad, sounds enterprise-y)
